Topographic Map Reading
üìà Contour Line Analysis:
  • Contour Lines: Connect points of equal elevation
  • Contour Interval: Vertical distance between lines
  • Close Lines: Steep slopes
  • Wide Lines: Gentle slopes
  • Circular Patterns: Hills or depressions
üîç Detective Skills - Elevation Analysis:
  1. Find the contour interval on the map legend
  2. Count contour lines to determine elevation changes
  3. Look for patterns that indicate landforms
  4. Use elevation to predict climate and vegetation
üìê Distance Measurement Methods
Method 1: Graphic Scale
1. Mark distance on paper edge
2. Compare to scale bar
3. Read actual distance
4. Account for map curves
Method 2: Grid Squares
1. Count grid squares
2. Multiply by grid scale
3. Add partial squares
4. Convert to desired units

Documentation Protocols
üìù The 5 W's + H Method:
  1. WHO: People involved, witnesses, suspects
  2. WHAT: Evidence found, geographic features observed
  3. WHEN: Time and date of discovery
  4. WHERE: Exact location with coordinates
  5. WHY: Significance to the investigation
  6. HOW: Method of discovery or collection

üìñ Geographic Vocabulary
Essential Geographic Terms
Absolute Location
Exact position on Earth's surface using coordinates (latitude/longitude)
Relative Location
Position described in relation to other places ("north of the school")
Cardinal Directions
The four main compass points: North, South, East, West
Intermediate Directions
Northeast, Southeast, Southwest, Northwest
Scale
Relationship between distance on a map and actual distance on Earth
Elevation
Height above sea level, measured in feet or meters
Topography
Physical features of Earth's surface (mountains, valleys, plains)
Climate
Long-term weather patterns of a region
GPS (Global Positioning System)
Satellite system that provides precise location coordinates
Investigation Terminology
Evidence
Information that helps prove or disprove something in an investigation
Hypothesis
Educated guess about what happened, based on evidence
Corroborate
To confirm information using multiple sources
Alibi
Proof that someone was elsewhere when something happened
Motive
Reason why someone might commit a crime
Witness
Person who saw or heard something related to the case
