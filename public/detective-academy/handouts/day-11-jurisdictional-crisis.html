üìè Understanding Maritime Boundaries

Learn how international maritime law defines territorial waters:

  1. Territorial Sea: 12 nautical miles from coastline - full sovereignty
  2. Contiguous Zone: 24 nautical miles - limited jurisdiction
  3. Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ): 200 nautical miles - resource rights
  4. Continental Shelf: Extended seabed - mineral and oil rights

üìú UN Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS)

Analyze how international maritime law applies to this dispute:

Key UNCLOS Principles:

  • Continental shelf extends to 200 nautical miles OR to the edge of continental margin
  • Countries can claim extended shelf beyond 200 miles with scientific evidence
  • Overlapping claims must be resolved through negotiation or arbitration
  • Environmental protection is required in all maritime zones

Evidence Requirements for Extended Continental Shelf Claims:

  1. Geological Evidence: Prove underwater landforms connect to your coast
  2. Scientific Surveys: Map the seafloor with detailed bathymetric data
  3. Sediment Analysis: Show sediment originates from your landmass
  4. Expert Review: UN Commission on Limits of Continental Shelf approval
